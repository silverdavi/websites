## ðŸš€ Program Synopsis

The **NSF SBIR/STTR programs** (America's Seed Fund powered by NSF) provide non-dilutive grants for the development of deep technologies based on discoveries in science and engineering with the potential for societal and economic impacts.

**Key Objectives:**
1.  **Innovation:** Fund unproven, leading-edge technology innovations.
2.  **R&D:** Support research that involves high technical risk and requires proof-of-concept.
3.  **Commercialization:** Translate discoveries into products/services with commercial and societal benefit.

**Exclusions:**
* No Clinical Trials.
* No Schedule I controlled substances (production/distribution).
* No evolutionary development or straightforward engineering (must be high-risk R&D).

## âœ… Eligibility Information

### General Requirements
* Must be a **Small Business Concern (SBC)**.
* Must be **independently owned and operated** (not dominant in the field).
* **Size:** 500 employees or fewer (including affiliates).
* **Location:** Work must be performed in the United States; company must be majority U.S.-owned.
* **Submission Limit:** Only **one** Project Pitch and/or Proposal per submission deadline.

### SBIR vs. STTR Differences

| Feature | SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research) | STTR (Small Business Tech Transfer) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Partner Requirement** | Partner optional. | **Mandatory** non-profit research institution partner. |
| **PI Employment** | PI must be >50% employed by the Small Business. | PI may be employed by Small Business OR Research Institution. |
| **Work Split** | Min 66% (2/3) by Small Business.<br>Max 33% (1/3) by Consultants/Subs. | Min 40% by Small Business.<br>Min 30% by Research Institution.<br>Remaining 30% flexible. |

## ðŸ“ Proposal Preparation Steps

### Step 1: The Project Pitch (Mandatory Pre-requisite)
* You **must** submit a Project Pitch and receive an official invitation via email to submit a full proposal.
* Pitches can be submitted at any time.
* Invitations are valid for **two** submission deadlines.

### Step 2: Full Proposal (Via Research.gov)
If invited, the full proposal must include:

#### A. Cover Sheet & Certifications
* Includes human subjects/vertebrate animals info.
* Certification regarding Malign Foreign Talent Recruitment Programs (CHIPS Act).

#### B. Project Summary (1 Page Max)
Must include three distinct headers:
1.  **Overview:** Potential outcome (product/service).
2.  **Intellectual Merit:** Technical hurdles and R&D goals.
3.  **Broader Impacts:** Societal and commercial impact.

#### C. Project Description (10â€“15 Pages)
Must include the following sections:
1.  **Intellectual Merit:** The innovation, technical risks, and R&D plan.
2.  **Company/Team:** Qualifications of the team and founders.
3.  **Broader Impacts:** Societal benefits (education, environment, economic competitiveness).
4.  **Commercialization Potential:** Market opportunity, business model, competition, and revenue plan.

> **Note:** Do not include URLs in the Project Description.

#### D. Budget & Justification
* Must not exceed $305,000.
* **Subawards:** Required for STTR; optional for SBIR.
* **Consultants:** Max rate $1,000/day.

#### E. Personnel Documents (Required for all Senior/Key Personnel)
* **Biographical Sketch:** Must use **SciENcv**.
* **Current and Pending Support:** Must use **SciENcv**.
* **Collaborators & Other Affiliations (COA):** Excel template.
* **Synergistic Activities:** 1-page document (up to 5 examples).

#### F. Supplementary Documents
* **Data Management Plan:** (Required).
* **Mentoring Plan:** Required if funding grad students/postdocs.
* **Letters of Commitment:** From subawardees/consultants.
* **Project Pitch Invitation:** Upload the email from NSF.
* **NO Letters of Support:** General letters of support are *not* allowed.

## ðŸ”Ž Merit Review Criteria

Proposals are reviewed on three criteria:

1.  **Intellectual Merit:**
    * Does it advance knowledge?
    * Is the plan creative, original, or transformative?
    * Is the R&D plan sound?

2.  **Broader Impacts:**
    * Benefit to society (STEM workforce, economic competitiveness, etc.).

3.  **Commercialization Potential:**
    * Is there a significant market opportunity?
    * Does the company have a competitive advantage (IP/Moat)?
    * Is the business model compelling?
