## Phase 1: Project Pitch

### Before Submitting Pitch

- [ ] Read full NSF 24-579 solicitation
- [ ] Identified which technology area fits (likely: Advanced Manufacturing, Robotics, or IoT)
- [ ] Drafted pitch text (problem/solution/innovation/team/market)
- [ ] Internal review of pitch
- [ ] Decided: SBIR or STTR?

### Pitch Content (1000 words max per section)

- [ ] **Technology Innovation:** What's the technical problem and your novel approach?
- [ ] **Technical Objectives:** What will you demonstrate in Phase I?
- [ ] **Broader Impacts:** Societal benefits, commercial potential
- [ ] **Team:** Key qualifications
- [ ] **Company:** Brief background

### Pitch Submission

- [ ] Submitted via seedfund.nsf.gov
- [ ] Received confirmation email
- [ ] **Invitation received:** ☐ Yes / ☐ No
- [ ] Invitation date: _______________
- [ ] Valid until (2 deadlines): _______________ and _______________

## Phase 3: Proposal Content

### Project Summary (1 page)

- [ ] **Overview:** Product/service outcome (not process)
- [ ] **Intellectual Merit:** Technical innovation and R&D challenges
- [ ] **Broader Impacts:** Societal and commercial benefits
- [ ] No jargon, standalone document
- [ ] Proofread

### Project Description (15 pages)

- [ ] **1. The Innovation** (2-3 pages)
  - [ ] Problem statement
  - [ ] Existing solutions and their limitations
  - [ ] Your approach and why it's novel
  - [ ] Key technical risks
  
- [ ] **2. Technical Objectives and Work Plan** (5-6 pages)
  - [ ] Objective 1: DSL design and safety model
  - [ ] Objective 2: LLM-assisted translator
  - [ ] Objective 3: Micro-VM on ESP32/Arduino
  - [ ] Objective 4: User validation
  - [ ] Timeline/Gantt chart
  - [ ] Deliverables and success metrics
  
- [ ] **3. Company and Team** (2-3 pages)
  - [ ] Company background
  - [ ] PI qualifications
  - [ ] Key personnel
  - [ ] Consultants and advisors
  - [ ] Facilities and equipment
  
- [ ] **4. Broader Impacts** (1-2 pages)
  - [ ] Societal benefits
  - [ ] Education/workforce
  - [ ] Diversity
  
- [ ] **5. Commercialization Plan** (3-4 pages)
  - [ ] Market opportunity
  - [ ] Target customers
  - [ ] Business model
  - [ ] Competition and differentiation
  - [ ] Revenue projections
  - [ ] Phase II and beyond plan

### Project Description Rules

- [ ] No URLs
- [ ] No references to appendices
- [ ] 1-inch margins minimum
- [ ] 11pt+ font
- [ ] Single-spaced is OK
- [ ] Page numbers included
- [ ] Figures/tables count toward page limit

### Budget Rules

- [ ] Total ≤ $305,000
- [ ] Small business does ≥66% of work
- [ ] Consultants/subs ≤33% of work
- [ ] Consultant rate ≤$1,000/day
- [ ] I-Corps ($25k) included
- [ ] TABA ($6.5k) included
- [ ] Every line item justified

## Phase 5: Review & Submit

### Internal Review

- [ ] Technical review (is the science right?)
- [ ] Compliance review (page limits, formatting)
- [ ] Budget review (math checks out)
- [ ] Spelling/grammar check
- [ ] Read from reviewer's perspective

### Red Team Questions

- [ ] Is the problem clearly stated?
- [ ] Is the innovation obvious?
- [ ] Are the risks addressed?
- [ ] Is the team credible?
- [ ] Is the market real?
- [ ] Would I fund this?

### Submission

- [ ] All documents uploaded to Research.gov
- [ ] PDF previews checked
- [ ] AOR (Authorized Org Rep) notified
- [ ] Submitted before 5:00 PM local time
- [ ] Confirmation email received
